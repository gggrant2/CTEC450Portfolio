About Genesis

I am anticipated to graduate with a Bachelors of Science in Computer Technology with a Concentration in Data Science and Database Administration, from Bowie State University in May 2025.

I have gained hands-on experience through internships at Bowie State University, the MITRE Corporation, and the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST), where I contributed to a range of technical projects involving AI security, embedded systems, and data analytics.

My technical background includes proficiency in Python, SQL, and data-focused libraries such as Pandas, NumPy, and Matplotlib, along with software like Jupyter Notebook, Tableau, and Excel. I have applied these skills in real-world projects, developing automated systems, analyzing large datasets, and working on solutions for prompt injection vulnerabilities in LLMs.

Highlighted Projects

The following projects represent a selection of coursework completed by me, within the Computer Technology program at Bowie State University. Each project highlights practical applications of data science, programming, and analytical skills gained through academic and internship experiences. Click on a project title to explore detailed examples of coursework, technical implementation, and creative design.

  • CTEC 298: Symbolic Computation Using Big Data

    • Within CTEC298Data Jupyter Notebook, I used Python and data wrangling libraries to import raw data for cleaning and organize it for analysis. I created visualizations to highlight key patterns and trends, then put together a final presentation to explain my findings and what the data revealed.

  • CTEC 402: Software and Operating System Security

    • For my final course project, I expanded my internship research on the security of large language models (LLMs). I explored vulnerabilities related to prompt injection and jailbreak attacks, conducted testing to evaluate these risks, and presented mitigation strategies aimed at strengthening model defenses.

  • CTEC 426: Expert Systems Using Big Data

    • The CTEC 426 Jupyter Notebook project analyzes wage disparities across groups using raw data from the 2024 Census Bureau. Using Python libraries, the data was cleaned and processed to generate visualizations that examine income distribution by race, gender, and industry, uncovering recent patterns and possible inequalities based on the most up-to-date and relevant information available.
